Young Greens vote to support European Citizens Initiative for a Human Right to Water

15 November 2012

At last weekend's national Convention Young Greens voted to support a campaign for a Human Right to Water. The campaign is being coordinated by the Federation of Young European Greens.

The full text of the motion:


National Convention Notes:
 
Since April 2012 One Million Citizens from at least 7 member states of the European Union can invite the European Commission to start a legislative proposal on a certain topic. This procedure is called European Citizens Initiative (ECI).
 
The European Citizens Initiative 'Water and Sanitation are a human right! Water is a public good, not a commodity.' has been launched in May 2012. Concrete demands of the initiative are:
 
“We invite the European Commission to propose legislation implementing the human right to water and sanitation as recognised by the United Nations, and promoting the provision of water and sanitation as essential public services for all. The EU legislation should require governments to ensure and to provide all citizens with sufficient and clean drinking water and sanitation. We urge that:

  1. The EU institutions and Member States be obliged to ensure that all inhabitants enjoy the right to water and sanitation.
  2. Water supply and management of water resources not be subject to ‘internal market rules’ and that water services are excluded from liberalisation.
  3. The EU increases its efforts to achieve universal access to water and sanitation.”


Between the launched initiatives the water initiative of the European Public Service Union and many other NGOs is the best organized one.
 
The Federation of Young European Greens (FYEG) developed a strategy for promoting ECIs. In order to promote an ECI, FYEG needs 10 Member Organisations on their side to officially support a campaign.
 

National Convention Believes:

  1. European Democracy should be improved.
  2. The European Citizens Initiative is not binding on the Commission, but still more than a petition. An infrastructure is provided to build up European Networks of Citizens. People need to work together across borders. This is the first step towards bottom-up Democratic reforms.
  3. Access to Water and Sanitation should be guaranteed for everybody in Europe and the world.
  4. Liberalisation of water leads to a poor infrastructure and high prices.

 
National Convention Resolves:
 

  1. YGs support the European Citizens Initiative 'Water and Sanitation are a human right! Water is a public good, not a commodity.'
  2. YGs take part in the FYEG campaign to promote the ECI 'Water and Sanitation are a human right! Water is a public good, not a commodity.'
  3. YGs nominate a contact person for the FYEG campaign.
  4. Efforts should be made to collect signatures for the campaign.
